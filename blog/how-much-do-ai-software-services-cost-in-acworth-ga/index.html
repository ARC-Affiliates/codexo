What Is the 30% Rule for AI?

The 30% rule for AI is an adoption heuristic suggesting AI should automate roughly 30% of a task's workload — not 100% — to maintain human oversight and quality.

The 30% rule means AI should handle the repetitive 30% of any workflow while humans handle the judgment-heavy 70%, maximizing both efficiency and accuracy.

This principle has gained traction among enterprise AI teams because fully automated systems produce hallucinations (AI-generated outputs that are plausible-sounding but factually wrong) and compliance risks. According to Codexo, the 30% framing helps Acworth clients scope realistic pilots — a Snellville local firm doesn't replace paralegals with AI; they automate document intake and let humans review. The rule also applies to budget: allocate about 30% of an AI project's total cost to ongoing monitoring, retraining, and governance, not just the initial build.

"Responsible AI deployment requires human oversight proportional to the risk of the decision being automated — full automation of high-stakes tasks without human review remains inappropriate." — National Institute of Standards and Technology, AI Risk Management Framework, nist.gov
