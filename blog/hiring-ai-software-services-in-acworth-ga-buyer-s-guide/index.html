What Are the 7 Types of AI Most Acworth Businesses Encounter?

The seven types of AI is a common taxonomy splitting AI by capability and function — important because pricing and vendor fit depend on which type you actually need.

The seven types are reactive machines, limited memory, theory of mind, self-aware AI, narrow AI (ANI), general AI (AGI), and superintelligent AI (ASI).

  • Reactive machines — rule-based systems with no memory (chess engines).
  • Limited memory — most current ML, including fraud detection and recommendation engines.
  • Theory of mind — research-stage systems modeling human emotions.
  • Self-aware AI — theoretical, not commercially available.
  • Narrow AI (ANI) — task-specific tools like chatbots and OCR.
  • General AI (AGI) — human-level reasoning, not yet achieved.
  • Superintelligent AI (ASI) — beyond human capability, theoretical.

For 99% of Acworth buyers, you are buying limited-memory or narrow AI in 2026. Anything else is marketing language. Predictive AI vs generative AI: predictive AI is the better choice for forecasting, scoring, and risk modeling because it produces measurable accuracy metrics. Generative AI is the tradeoff choice for content drafting, summarization, and code assist because outputs are creative but harder to validate.
