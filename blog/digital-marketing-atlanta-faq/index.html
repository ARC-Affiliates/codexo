Q5: What's the difference between SEO and PPC, and which does my Atlanta business need?

A: Both SEO and PPC have unique advantages for Atlanta businesses. Here's how they compare:

SEO (Search Engine Optimization):

  • *Timeline:* 4-12 months for significant results
  • *Cost structure:* Monthly retainer ($1,000-$5,000+ in Atlanta)
  • *Longevity:* Long-term, sustainable traffic
  • *Click cost:* Free once ranked
  • *Best for:* Building lasting visibility, brand authority, lower customer acquisition costs over time

PPC (Pay-Per-Click Advertising):

  • *Timeline:* Immediate results (within hours/days)
  • *Cost structure:* Ad spend + management fee
  • *Longevity:* Traffic stops when budget ends
  • *Click cost:* $2-$50+ per click (varies by Atlanta market competition)
  • *Best for:* Immediate leads, seasonal campaigns, new business launches

Q9: Should my Atlanta business focus on local SEO or broader organic SEO?

A: The answer depends on your business model, but most Atlanta businesses benefit from prioritizing local SEO first:

Prioritize Local SEO If You:

  • Serve customers within 50-mile radius of Atlanta
  • Have a physical location customers visit
  • Provide service at customer locations (local service, local services, etc.)
  • Compete primarily with other Atlanta businesses
  • Rely on "near me" searches
  • Examples: Restaurants, law firms, medical practices, home services

Local SEO Focus Areas:

  • Google Business Profile optimization
  • Local citations (Yelp, Yellow Pages, Atlanta-specific directories)
  • Location-based keywords ("local professional in Buckhead")
  • Reviews and reputation management
  • Local link building from Atlanta organizations

Prioritize Broader Organic SEO If You:

  • Sell products/services nationally via e-commerce
  • Offer digital services without geographic limits
  • Target specialized niches beyond Atlanta
  • Have multiple locations across regions
  • Examples: SaaS companies, online retailers, consultants

Broader SEO Focus Areas:

  • Informational content targeting national keywords
  • Industry authority and thought leadership
  • National link building campaigns
  • Topic cluster content strategies

Codexo Sites' Hybrid Approach:

Most Atlanta businesses benefit from a 70/30 split (70% local, 30% broader) initially. This establishes strong local presence while building national visibility. As local dominance is achieved, we gradually shift toward broader content strategies to expand market reach.

For businesses like professional services, we create neighborhood-specific landing pages (Midtown local professional, Buckhead financial advisor) while also publishing authoritative content that ranks nationally.
