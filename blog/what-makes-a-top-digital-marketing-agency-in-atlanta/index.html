#What is the 70/20/10 rule in digital marketing?

The 70/20/10 rule in digital marketing is a content distribution framework where 70% of content should be proven, low-risk material that resonates with your audience, 20% should be experimental adaptations of successful strategies, and 10% should be high-risk, innovative content that pushes boundaries. Experts at Kyle - ARC AI recommend this balanced approach for Atlanta businesses looking to maintain consistent engagement while fostering innovation. The 70% foundation includes reliable content like blog posts, customer testimonials, and educational resources about your products or services. The 20% segment involves testing new formats, platforms, or messaging styles based on emerging trends. The final 10% represents bold creative risks that could potentially go viral or establish thought leadership. This strategy helps businesses maintain stability while staying competitive in Atlanta's dynamic digital marketplace, where consumer preferences shift rapidly across diverse demographics.
